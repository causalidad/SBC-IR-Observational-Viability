# Future Directions

The present manuscript identifies an observationally viable infrared perturbative regime.

A natural next step is to determine whether projection-based causal-memory constructions can reproduce this regime.

## Central question

Given a hidden-sector projection framework with finite causal accessibility, what classes of memory kernels naturally emerge, and which of them are capable of reproducing the observationally viable infrared cosmological regime identified in the present work?

## Open problems

1. Classify admissible causal-memory kernels.
2. Determine whether exponentially screened kernels arise naturally.
3. Study finite sums or distributions of relaxation modes.
4. Compare exponential, multi-exponential, and long-memory kernels.
5. Map kernel families to effective infrared responses F(z).
6. Assess whether the observed proxy-posterior region can be reproduced naturally.
