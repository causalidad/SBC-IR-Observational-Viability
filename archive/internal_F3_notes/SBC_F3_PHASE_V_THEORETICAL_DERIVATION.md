# SBC/F3 Phase V — Theoretical Derivation of the IR-safe Sector

## Objective

The observational phase identified a robust viable region:

epsilon ≈ 0.06
k0 ≈ 0.015–0.022
n ≈ 1

The goal of Phase V is to explain why this region may emerge naturally from the SBC framework.

---

# Core hypothesis

The SBC/F3 sector is interpreted as an effective infrared perturbative response generated by cosmological memory.

Instead of modifying the homogeneous background expansion, the viable branch acts as:

LCDM background
+
SBC/F3 perturbative infrared sector

---

# Memory-kernel origin

A minimal SBC memory correction can be represented as an effective convolution:

Delta_X(z) = ∫ K(z,z') R_X(z') dz'

where:

- K(z,z') is a causal memory kernel,
- R_X(z') is a response functional of the cosmological perturbation sector,
- Delta_X(z) is the effective SBC deformation of the observable X.

For an exponentially screened infrared kernel:

K(z,z') ~ exp[-k0 |z-z'|]

the late-time effective response naturally takes the approximate form:

F(z) ~ -epsilon exp[-k0(1+z)]

which corresponds to:

n ≈ 1.

---

# Interpretation of n ≈ 1

The repeated emergence of n ≈ 1 suggests that the observationally preferred SBC/F3 branch behaves like a first-order memory screening law.

This is analogous to a Markovian or near-Markovian infrared response:

memory weight ∝ exp[-scale]

rather than a higher-order ultraviolet-sensitive deformation.

Therefore:

n ≈ 1

can be interpreted as evidence for a linear infrared memory-decay regime.

---

# Interpretation of k0

The parameter k0 controls the characteristic infrared screening scale.

The preferred range:

k0 ≈ 0.015–0.022

indicates that the correction acts only over very large cosmological scales.

This explains why:

- BAO is preserved,
- Pantheon+ is preserved,
- high-ell CMB is preserved,
- perturbative low-ell sectors can still be affected.

---

# Interpretation of epsilon

The amplitude:

epsilon ≈ 0.06

sets the strength of the effective SBC perturbative response.

Its small value explains why the model remains observationally viable:

- it avoids strong background deformation,
- it avoids high-ell acoustic disruption,
- it preserves growth constraints,
- it produces only weak IR deviations.

---

# Theoretical classification

The viable SBC/F3 branch should be understood as:

a weak, exponentially screened, infrared memory-response sector

rather than:

a background-level alternative to LCDM.

---

# Physical summary

The observed phenomenology can be summarized as:

1. Cosmological memory generates an effective IR response.
2. Exponential memory screening produces n ≈ 1.
3. The response amplitude is small, epsilon ≈ 0.06.
4. The characteristic scale is deep infrared, k0 ≈ 0.015–0.022.
5. BAO and Pantheon+ force the homogeneous background to remain LCDM-like.
6. Observable SBC/F3 effects survive mainly in the perturbative sector.
